# Twitter Thread by @devhustler_raj (15 tweets)

1/ ok real talk nobody tells u this but the first yr of freelancing is basically paying tuition to the universe. u will undercharge, overdeliver, and wonder why ur broke. its normal. heres what i learned after 3 yrs 🧵

2/ lesson 1: STOP saying yes to everything. i used to take any project that came my way - wordpress sites, logo design, python scripts, whatever. jack of all trades = master of getting underpaid 💀

3/ the moment i niched down to just react + node backends my income literally 3x'd in 6 months. not even kidding. specialization > generalization every single time

4/ lesson 2: ur first client aint gonna come from upwork or fiverr. mine came from answering a question on stackoverflow lmaooo. dude was stuck on a cors issue, i helped, he dmed me on twitter, boom first $500 project 🚀

5/ lesson 3: charge per project NOT per hour. hourly = u get punished for being fast. i used to finish 20hr projects in 8hrs and felt guilty charging for 20. DONT BE ME

6/ real example: client wanted an ecommerce dashboard. quoted $3000 flat. finished in 2 weeks (~60hrs). thats $50/hr. if i charged $30/hr woulda made $1800 for same work 📉

7/ lesson 4: contracts. CONTRACTS. CONTRACTSSSS. i got burned TWICE before i learned. client ghosted after 80% completion. $2400 worth of work. gone. poof. 👻

8/ now i use a simple template - 50% upfront, milestones, kill clause if ghosted >14 days. saved my butt multiple times

9/ lesson 5: twitter (x) > linkedin for finding dev clients. controversial take but hear me out. on linkedin everyones trying to sell. on twitter ppl actually share problems u can solve

10/ my best clients came from: building in public threads (3), helping in DMs (4), one literal shitpost that went viral (1). no cap

11/ lesson 6: the "im not ready" feeling never goes away. i still feel it before every new project. the diff btwn yr 1 and yr 3 is yr 3 me knows its just anxiety not truth

12/ lesson 7: savings buffer or die. keep 3 months expenses in savings before going full time freelance. i didnt do this and ate instant ramen for a month straight when a client delayed payment 🍜

13/ ppl ask abt my current stack: next.js + tailwind + supabase for most projects. sometimes react native if mobile needed. keeps things simple, clients dont care abt ur stack they care if it works

14/ biggest regret: not starting a blog/newsletter sooner. content = long term lead gen. every post i write brings in ~2-3 leads per month now. compound interest but for clients 📈

15/ tldr: niche down, charge flat rates, get contracts, build in public, save money, start writing. freelancing aint easy but 3 yrs in im making more than my prev corp job working half the hrs. worth it? absolutely. dm me if u have qs ✌️
